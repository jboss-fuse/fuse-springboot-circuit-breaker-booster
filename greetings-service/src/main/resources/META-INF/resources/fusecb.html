Circuit Breaker - Red Hat Fuse

Cloud applications are often comprised of many services. Between those services there can be many interactions. In case a service fails or is unavailable, you can provide an alternative response that handles the error condition without increasing service load. To achieve this, you can implement a circuit breaker. A circuit breaker is a generic mechanism that can quickly and easily determine that a service is not functioning properly and then can either return a default result or a quick and graceful fail notice with detailed information about the failure.

When you open this page, it invokes the Greeting Service (which is backed by Apache Camel). This Greeting Service uses a name service. The invocations are protected by a circuit breaker that uses the camel-hystrix component.

You can stop, start, and clear the calls to the Greeting Service on this page.

To monitor the state of the circuit breaker, use the Hystrix Monitor dashboard.

To demonstrate the behavior of the circuit breaker when a service becomes unavailable:

  • In the OpenShift console, stop the name service by terminating the process or by downscaling the pod number to 0.
